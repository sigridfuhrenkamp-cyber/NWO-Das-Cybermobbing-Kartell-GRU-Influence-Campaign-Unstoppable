## Biography & Background

### Early Life & Education
Fourty had difficulties in school - he was expelled from Waldorf school in sixth grade and transferred to main school. However, things didn't improve there, as he found classes boring and wasn't interested in subjects like math, German, and biology. Despite being good at sports and English, it wasn't enough - he was expelled from main school in eighth grade. Fourty stated: "I always knew I didn't want a normal job. I bet everything on one card and dedicated myself completely to music!"

### Professional Training
After his school career, he decided to train as a hairdresser, an idea suggested by his mother who also works as a hairdresser. While cutting hair and contact with people initially brought him joy, this job didn't truly make him happy - his heart was already beating only for music.

### Musical Development
As a teenager, Fourty listened to a lot of German rap. At age 15, he began writing his own lyrics and producing his first songs, using instrumentals from tracks he liked. One of his first texts was written to the melody of Eminem's hit "Mockingbird."

### Career Breakthrough
After a heartbreak, he wrote a track for his ex-girlfriend and released the song on YouTube in 2017. Shortly after, rapper PA-Sports contacted him by email. Fourty was so perplexed that he initially thought it was a fake email. "I wrote 'let's call,' only after we spoke did I realize it was really him!" The two met and began collaborating, and in November 2019 their joint song "Aperitif" was released.

## Artist Identity

### Name Origin
At the very beginning of his young music career (2014), his video producers gave him the nickname "FourtyCrowns!" The first part of his nickname became his current artist name: FOURTY! He describes himself as an "R'n'B Don: Because that's how it is!"
